SWOT Analysis

Internal strengths/weaknesses and external opportunities/threats

S

Strengths

91% owner satisfaction (JD Power). Tech leadership: Autopilot, FSD, OTA. Vertical integration: 4680 battery cells. Direct sales eliminates $2,000+ dealer markup. 15,000+ Supercharger network

W

Weaknesses

Quality control: panel gaps reported by 23% of owners. Limited service centers: avg 45-min drive vs 15-min for legacy OEMs. High CEO brand dependency — PR volatility risk

O

Opportunities

US EV market projected $67B by 2026 (Statista). $7,500 IRA federal tax credit since 2023. Autonomous driving TAM: $300B+ by 2030 (Goldman Sachs). Fleet/commercial expansion

T

Threats

BYD surpassed Tesla in global EV sales (Q4 2023). Legacy OEMs investing $500B+ in EV transition. Lithium prices rose 300% in 2022. Potential tax credit phase-out risk

Marketing Mix — 4Ps

Current marketing mix strategy analysis

P1

Product Strategy

Price Strategy

$38,990-$52,990 range. Transparent pricing, zero dealer markup. $7,500 tax credit. Saves $6,000-8,000 over 5 years vs BMW 3 Series (Edmunds)

Place Strategy

100% direct-to-consumer. Online ordering in 8 min avg. 200+ Tesla stores in premium retail locations. 15,000+ Superchargers, NACS adopted as US standard

Promotion Strategy

Recommendations

4Ps, 7Ps & STP strategy recommendations

Marketing Mix (4Ps + 7Ps)

Launch sub-$30K variant to capture 60% of priced-out market
Implement dynamic pricing based on demand signals
Expand service centers by 40% (current wait: 2-3 weeks)
Targeted digital campaigns on YouTube/TikTok for Gen Z
7Ps: Improve service training (ACSI below avg), streamline mobile service, redesign showrooms with VR test drives

STP Strategy

Broaden target to mid-income ($50-75K HHI)
Strengthen positioning vs Chinese EVs (BYD, MG4)
Develop fleet/B2B segment for corporate ESG goals
Expand female targeting (currently only 28% women)
Position as 'best value in class' not 'cheapest Tesla'
Create subscription tier for younger buyers
